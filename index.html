Ownership identification

How to tell SDG&E and CIP-owned poles apart

AT&T owned poles with SDG&E attachments are identified with a “J” suffix in Geocall.

These poles should show a “J” suffix on the SDG&E tag, while the AT&T tag may use a “JP” prefix.

CIP-owned poles placed inter-set between SDG&E poles need extra attention during inspection.

Inspection requirement

Clearances to inspect on inter-set CIP poles

Check ground clearances of the CIP conductors on poles located between SDG&E poles.

Also inspect wire-to-wire clearances to make sure spacing remains safe and compliant.

These checks are especially important where CIP conductors pass through spans influenced by nearby SDG&E structures.

Condition assessment

Signs a CIP pole may be stressing SDG&E poles

Look for a CIP pole that is leaning and transferring force to adjacent SDG&E poles.

Check for damage that could create unnecessary strain on the connected line path.

If the inter-set CIP pole appears unstable, note the issue and evaluate how it may affect both support and clearance.
